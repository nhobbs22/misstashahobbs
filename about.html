About Me

I received my Bachelors in Studio Art as well as a minor musical theater at Santa Clara University back in 2012. I also received my Masters in Teaching at Santa Clara in 2022. Go Broncos! My Teaching Philosophy

“There is a time in every man's education when he arrives at the conviction that envy is ignorance; that imitation is suicide; that he must take himself for better, for worse, as his portion; [...] The power which resides in him is new in nature, and none but he knows what that is which he can do, nor does he know until he has tried.” - Ralph Waldo Emerson

    Each and every one of us have our own bit of magic, and I believe that is my responsibility as a future educator to help find what makes a child sparkle and encourage that child to shine to their brightest potential. No two students work the same way and what motivates each individual is very different. I want to create a classroom experience tailored to help students’ individualities and differences shine. This can be accomplished by creating a supportive and inclusive community in my classroom where students feel safe to express their ideas.

    I am devoted to teaching because of my overall love and faith in humanity. It is my goal to create compassionate, virtuous learners who see the benefits of helping the greater good. I want to foster competent members of society that can challenge the norms and can contribute to its growth. This can be accomplished by allowing students the opportunity to be diverse learners and by emphasizing the importance of building a supportive community in the classroom. I strive to create an environment where students are not limited to “imitation” but are encouraged to find their own voice. I seek to understand what drives each child’s mind and inspires him or her. I am excited to learn about what unique brilliance my students have to offer by giving them the confidence to believe in themselves and the tools to learn how to think, question, and grow in our inclusive classroom environment. Through building a symbiotic community, we can teach empathy, affirmations, and self-reliance to children.


It is important to provide children with both the academic and emotional skills needed to navigate this world. It is so crucial that such skills be fostered so that the next generations value both themselves and each other thus be well placed to take on the challenge of stewarding human existence. I have faith that all children are capable of greatness. Every human deserves a chance to be the best version of themselves and I am committed to provide children with the means to reach their true potential.

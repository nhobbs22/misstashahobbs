Our classroom

Classroom Demographics

Our classroom this year consists of 24 wonderfully diverse students! We are so lucky to have students of various ethnic backgrounds ranging from 7 Latin American students, 8 White students, 5 African American students, and 4 Asian American students . There are 13 girls and 11 boys in our class this year. Our students speak various languages and I am excited to include all of them in our lessons. We have: English, Spanish, and Japanese.


Classroom Layout

Our classroom is set up in a way that promotes discussion and community. Students' desks are placed together to encourage small group collaboration. We also have a carpeted area devoted to restorative circle discussion. Conversation is an important part of our classroom because it promotes cooperative learning. Cooperative learning helps increase engagement and participation. It also teaches project management skills that students will directly see in the workforce. These skills include: leadership skills, social skills, problem solving skills, and communication skills. Another important part about our classroom layout is our Mindfulness corner. You will learn more about mindfulness in our Routines & Procedures section as well as our Resources section. Our library will be full of all sorts of books that support English Language Learners and promote diversity in the classroom.


Daily Routine
Mindul Minute

One thing you may notice on our schedule is the Mindful Minute placed in between classes. Implementing mindful learning in the classroom means teaching students to establish healthy habits of the mind early on in life. Research has shown that teaching children mindfulness practices can help children be happier, seem more comfortable in their body, and become less reactive or aggressive. Introducing very short periods of practice repeatedly throughout the day will help calm children's nervous system and regain their focus throughout the day.

Procedures
Morning Procedure

I will greet the students every morning as they enter the classroom. The students will get out their homework from their homework folder, place it in the turn-it-in bin, and then put away their backpacks in their cubbies. They will then sit at their desks and play with their play dough until we are ready to begin. To start out the day, we will always begin with our daily affirmation followed by our daily calendar read. We will then have our mindful minute to center ourselves for our learning.

Afternoon Procedure

We will end our day by cleaning up the classroom together starting with our own desks. We will then sit down for our restorative circle. Here we will introduce read alouds that stimulate social-emotional conversation. We will discuss various emotions we feel and learn strategies on how to effectively manage those emotions. We will also end the day sharing our rose, our bud, and our thorn: One challenge we experienced, a positive we experienced, and something we look forward to tomorrow or in the upcoming week.

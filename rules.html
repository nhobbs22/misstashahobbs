Conduct

Classroom Rules
Why Rules?

Rules help provide children with a guidline of what to expect in the classroom. Since we have a large class of 24 students, it is important that children understand how to be respectful, responsible, and safe in the classroom. I will go over our class rules on the first day and provide a printed visual for parents so they too understand what is expected of their child.

Why Diversity

There are many different reasons why it is important to teach diversity. First and foremost, diversity teaches us how to love, respect, and appreciate others who are different than us. By allowing your mind to understand differences, you are actually increasing the neuroplasticy in your brain. By understand diversity, you open yourself up to a whole new realm of possibilities.

Consequences

Positive Consequences

Psychology has proven that humans respond much better to positive vs. negative reinforcement. That is why in my classroom I focus on rewarding students for good behavior. One reward system I specifically have in place in my classroom is our “Sweet for Sweets” system. This is where the children in the class are rewarded points for their acts of kindness. Once we hit a certain number of points, I will bake the class sweets. Kindness is essential for making all students feel safe in the classroom. I have included more further resources on the power of empathy in our resources page if you would like to learn more.


Negative Consequences

Unfortunately there are times where positive reinforcement is not enough to navigate behavior. When our class is acting out as a whole, or there is conflict between multiple students. I will take away points from our reward system, but provide them with a solution to win them back. If an individual student is not following class rules, the student will be required to fill out a “Think Sheet” reflecting on their behavior. If the same behavior happens twice in once week, they must take the think sheet home to their parents and have a discussion about how their actions can be better managed. If this problem still cannot be resolved, we will have a parent-teacher conference discussing why your child may be acting out in order to brainstorm solutions.
